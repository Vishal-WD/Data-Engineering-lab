# Data Engineering Overview

## What is Data Engineering?

Data Engineering is the discipline of designing, building, and maintaining systems that collect data from multiple sources, transform it into reliable and high-quality datasets, and deliver it to downstream users such as Data Analysts, Data Scientists, Machine Learning Engineers, Business Intelligence teams, and applications.

A Data Engineer ensures that data is accurate, scalable, secure, and available whenever the business needs it.

---

## Why is Data Engineering Important?

Modern organizations generate enormous amounts of data every second.

Examples include:

- Customer transactions
- Mobile applications
- Websites
- IoT devices
- Payment systems
- ERP systems
- CRM platforms

This data is often stored across multiple systems in different formats.

Without Data Engineering:

- Reports become inconsistent.
- Business decisions become unreliable.
- Machine Learning models perform poorly.
- Analysts spend most of their time cleaning data instead of analyzing it.

Data Engineering solves these problems by creating reliable data pipelines.

---

## Responsibilities of a Data Engineer

A modern Data Engineer is responsible for:

- Collecting data from multiple sources.
- Building ETL/ELT pipelines.
- Cleaning and validating data.
- Designing scalable data models.
- Managing Data Lakes and Lakehouses.
- Processing batch and streaming data.
- Building workflow automation.
- Monitoring production pipelines.
- Optimizing data processing performance.
- Ensuring data quality and reliability.
